As a resident of Brixton I have seen my home change over the years and felt moved to act to preserve the place I call home. I decided to join forces with a group of neighbours all united to stop the eviction of local businesses and communities. The last straw being the eviction attempt on our beloved Nour Cash & Carry. Nour Cash'n Carry, a shop in Brixton Market, has been providing staples for a multi-cultural neighbourhood for 30 years. Even so, the beloved shop was threatened with eviction by Taylor McWilliams of Hondo Enterprises, the market's new landlord who dabbles as a DJ. Loyal patrons started to organise. Street protests were not possible due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Instead, they gatecrashed McWilliams' online DJ set during a flamboyant Zoom party hosted by the well-known Pacha club in Ibiza [reports 1 | 2]. From then on, support for the shop turned into an organised campaign to Save Nour [Instagram | Facebook | YouTube]. The group liaised with the Shaheen family, tweeted, released videos, launched a petition, raised ¬£10,000 in 24 hours [1 | 2] raising to over 20,000, and eventually announced that Nour would remain in the market after all. The website www.savenour.com became the platform for the campaign to #FightTheTower, another project by Hondo.

The campaign was supported by mainstream media and many creatives and cultural figures, for instance from the independent music scene, including musicians, venues, promoters and punters: [New Statesman | Quietus | Resident Advisor | djmag | Magnetic Magazine | TimeOut | Eater | Vice [1 | 2 | 3] | Amit Lennon | The Face | Independent | i-news | Dummy].
